What we're tracking and why

p(Doom)1 is a small independent project. We track aggregate signals to understand whether the game is actually reaching people who care about AI Safety — not to profile individual users. All metrics are aggregate-only: no personal data, no cross-site tracking, no cookies.

We use Plausible Analytics (self-hosted on analytics.pdoom1.com), which is open-source, GDPR-compliant, and publishes only anonymous, aggregated counts. You can opt out at any time using the button below.

What we do not track

We will never collect or store:

  • Individual user identifiers or persistent cookies
  • In-game session data, save files, or playtime per person
  • Email addresses, names, or any personally identifiable information
  • Device fingerprints, cross-site behaviour, or advertising IDs
  • Location data beyond country-level (already anonymised by Plausible)

Measurement roadmap

Signals we plan to add as the project grows:

Near-term

  • Plausible page-view tracking on all pages
  • File-download events for game releases
  • Outbound link click tracking
  • Public Plausible dashboard (read-only share link)
  • Weekly automated metrics digest in the Dev Blog

Medium-term

  • In-game opt-in crash reporting (aggregate only)
  • Session-length distribution (no individual IDs)
  • Which game ending players reach most often
  • Forum engagement metrics (posts per week, active topics)

Long-term

  • Pre/post survey on AI Safety knowledge (voluntary, anonymous)
  • Grant progress milestones linked to measured traction
  • Open data export — all aggregate metrics published as JSON
